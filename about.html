UDF History

The United Democratic Front (UDF) was an anti-apartheid body that incorporated many anti-apartheid organisations. It was launched in 1983, in Mitchells Plain.

Steps towards forming the UDF began in the late 1970s, and moved forward when Allan Boesak called for a 'united front' of 'churches, civic associations, trade unions, student organisations, and sports bodies' to fight oppression. He was addressing the Transvaal Anti-SAIC Committee (TASC) at a conference in Johannesburg on 23 January 1983, but this part of his speech was unplanned- and even he was surprised to see the result.

Our mission & vision

Our mission is to promote democracy, promote unity, and drive progressive change for the betterment of all citizens.

01
Socioeconomic Empowerment

This entails implementing policies and programs aimed at improving the economic well-being and opportunities for marginalized communities. It involves intiatives like job creation, access to education and healthcare, and support for small businesses and entreprenuers.

02
Electoral Reform

This involves advocating for changes in the electoral system to ensure fairness, transparency, and equal representation for all citizens. It may include initiatives such as elecoral law amendments, ensuring free and fair elections, and combating voter suppression tactics.

03
Addressing Systematic inequalities

Focusing on identifying and dismantling systematic barriers that perpetuate inequality and discrimmination based on factors susch as race, gender, or socioeconomic status. Advocating for policy changes, implementing affirmative action programs and promoting inclusivity and diversity in all aspects of society.
